CPU

The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is often called the brain of the computer. It performs all the logical and arithmetic operations required to execute programs.

  • Responsible for processing instructions from programs.
  • Measured by clock speed (GHz) and number of cores.
  • Two main brands: Intel and AMD.

Modern CPUs contain multiple cores, allowing them to perform multiple tasks simultaneously for better performance and efficiency.

The performance of a CPU directly affects how fast applications and games run on your computer.

RAM

RAM (Random Access Memory) is the temporary storage used by the system to store data for active processes.

  • Volatile memory – contents are lost when the system is powered off.
  • Measured in gigabytes (GB) and affects multitasking capability.
  • DDR4 and DDR5 are common modern types of RAM.

More RAM allows smoother multitasking and faster performance in applications that require large amounts of data.

Insufficient RAM can cause your system to slow down or freeze during heavy workloads.

print("RAM helps your system run multiple programs efficiently!")
GPU

The GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) handles rendering images, videos, and 3D graphics on your computer.

  • Essential for gaming, video editing, and machine learning tasks.
  • Can be integrated (built into CPU) or dedicated (separate hardware).
  • Major manufacturers: NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel.

Dedicated GPUs contain their own memory (VRAM) which improves graphics performance significantly.

GPUs are also widely used in AI and deep learning due to their high parallel processing capabilities.

print("GPUs bring visuals and AI computations to life!")
Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board that connects all computer components together.

  • Contains sockets and slots for CPU, RAM, GPU, and storage devices.
  • Includes chipset, power connectors, and data buses.
  • Determines compatibility of components (like CPU socket type).

It acts as the communication backbone, ensuring data flows smoothly between all components.

High-end motherboards offer better build quality, more expansion slots, and advanced connectivity options.

print("The motherboard connects all parts of your PC together!")
Storage (SSD,HDD)

Storage devices hold your operating system, programs, and personal files.

  • HDDs (Hard Disk Drives) use spinning disks to read/write data.
  • SSDs (Solid State Drives) use flash memory and are much faster.
  • NVMe SSDs offer the best performance via PCIe interface.

Upgrading from an HDD to an SSD can drastically reduce boot and load times.

Storage capacity is measured in gigabytes (GB) or terabytes (TB), depending on user needs.

print("Fast storage makes your computer feel brand new!")
PSU

The PSU (Power Supply Unit) provides power to all components of the computer.

  • Converts AC power from the wall into DC power used by internal components.
  • Measured in watts (W); common ratings are 450W–850W.
  • Modular PSUs allow better cable management and airflow.

A reliable PSU ensures stable performance and protects components from power surges.

Choosing an efficient PSU (80+ Bronze, Silver, Gold certified) reduces electricity wastage and heat.

print("A good PSU powers your PC safely and efficiently!")
